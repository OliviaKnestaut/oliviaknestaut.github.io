Personal Statement

Puzzle pieces scattered across tables and floors have been a common sight my whole life: beautiful images created from piles of cardboard scraps. Year after year, assembling the same puzzles, my excitement never wanes. I have always hoped to find an area of study as captivating as puzzles, but nothing ever seemed to fit. Math satisfied the problem-solving desires but lacked creativity. Art satisfied my creative desires but fell short of a challenge. Looking for the right fit, I applied to an arts high school where I was accepted for Digital Communication Arts. The program allowed me to learn about and work with many different media types, but I quickly fell in love with design. I was able to explore all the ways I could use my math-oriented brain to create uniform and stylized artwork. I was confident that I wanted to study design until my Digital Media II class began a unit in computer science. The connections it drew between design and math, creativity and problem solving were like nothing else. Lines of code fit like puzzle pieces; strings of letters and symbols became working projects. Sadly, the pandemic brought the unit to a halt. Motivated to understand the content, I requested my teacher’s slides and taught myself the rest of the lessons at home.

Throughout the rest of high school, I narrowed my focus to the intersection of design and computer science. My school’s digital media track gave me some freedom to explore this while learning skills in the Adobe Suite. I have even grown beyond this curriculum through clubs like Technology Student Association and Communication Arts Honor Society. Many TSA events, like Webmaster, allowed me to combine multiple of my budding skills in one project. My work in TSA won me several state-level awards and even a few trips to nationals. CAHS provided me with volunteer experience which allowed me to learn about the real-world application of my digital media skills. I have been able to design promotional materials for clubs, live stream and film events, and more. As much as I love technology, it is something I struggle with daily. As a student with chronic migraines and sensory processing issues, one of my main goals is to get involved with accessibility in media. Time and time again I have faced technology-induced migraines and sensory overload. I want to use my education in computer science and media design to encourage the consideration of a range of disabilities throughout the process of media creation. I want the projects I design to be an example that accommodations can improve media for everyone. Upon the foundation I developed throughout high school, I plan to build towards mastery of computer science and design and help add meaningful media to the world.
